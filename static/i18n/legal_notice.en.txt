Risk, Outcomes, and Legal Terms

This platform may include active network scanning, service detection, port discovery, and related technical validation steps.
Such operations may generate log records on target systems, trigger security alerts,
cause performance degradation, or lead to interruptions in misconfigured environments.
For this reason, before starting the scan steps, the scope, time window, impact level, and rollback plan
must be clearly defined.

1) Authorization and Usage Boundary
- Use this platform only on systems you own or in environments where you have explicit written permission.
- Do not perform unauthorized tests against third-party networks, production systems, or publicly accessible services.
- Targeting systems beyond the approved scope may be considered an unauthorized access attempt.
- Where necessary, keep internal approval, contracts, pentest scope documents, and change records.

2) Operational Risks
- Intensive scanning may consume bandwidth and strain network devices.
- IDS/IPS, WAF, SIEM, or EDR systems may interpret the scan as hostile activity.
- Incorrect parameter selections may cause timeouts, delays, or temporary access issues in services.
- Multiple targets, broad CIDR ranges, or aggressive speed settings significantly increase the risk level.

3) Data Security and Privacy
- Collected technical outputs (IP, port, banner, service version, etc.) may be considered sensitive information.
- Share results only with authorized persons; apply masking or anonymization when needed.
- Store output files in secure storage and apply access controls against unauthorized access.
- Handle log records that may contain personal data in accordance with applicable regulations.

4) Interpretation of Results
- Scan outputs are not definitive proof, but a technical indicator.
- False positives and false negatives are possible; verify critical findings with a second method.
- Do not make a final security decision based on a single tool or a single parameter set.
- Clearly state assumptions, scope, and limitations during the reporting phase.

5) Legal and Organizational Obligations
- Local laws, organizational policy, and contractual obligations take priority.
- Unauthorized access, discovery, or testing attempts may lead to legal and criminal liability.
- The capabilities provided by this platform should be used for defense, validation, and improvement.
- The user accepts responsibility for the actions they perform.

6) Safe Working Practices
- Start with low-impact settings first, then increase them in a controlled manner when needed.
- If possible, test in an isolated lab environment first, then move to the target environment.
- Avoid aggressive scanning during critical times such as high-traffic or operational hours.
- Keep a record of the start time, scope, parameters, and results for each test session.

By checking the approval box, you declare that you have read,
understood, and will act only within authorized/approved usage of these risks, limits, and responsibilities.
